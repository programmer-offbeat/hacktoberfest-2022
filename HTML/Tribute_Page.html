Sir Isaac Newton

Newton proposed that planetary orbits are mostly elliptical because of the Sun’s gravitation. The Principia contains the law of universal gravitation and the three Newtonian laws of motion. The law of universal gravitation states that every particle of matter in the universe attracts every other particle with a force that is proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers. The first Newtonian law of motion is the principle of inertia, stating a body continues in its state of rest or of uniform motion in a straight line, unless it is compelled to change that state by forces impressed upon it. The second law states that the change of motion of an object is proportional to the force impressed and is made in the direction of the straight line in which the force is impressed. The third law states that to every action there is an equal and opposite reaction. These principles are the groundwork for all modern understanding of movement and physics.

Here's a timeline of Isaac Newton's life:

  • 1642: Born in Woolsthorpe Manor, United Kingdom.
  • 1661: admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge on the recommendation of his uncle Rev William Ayscough, who had studied there.
  • 1664: He was awarded a scholarship guaranteeing him four more years until he could get his MA.
  • 1667: returned to Cambridge and received his MA and in October was elected as a fellow of Trinity.
  • 1668: Newton invented the reflecting telescope.
  • 1672: He was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society(FRS).
  • 1687:He published three laws of motion, i.e, Law of Inertia, Law of Force and Acceleration and law of action and reaction.
  • 1703: Newton was elected president of the Royal Society.
  • 1704: He published the first edition of Opticks, a work summarizing his research in light and colors.
  • 1727: Newton died in London, England, and was buried at Westminster Abbey.
